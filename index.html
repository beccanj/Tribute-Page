Skillet

Awake and Alive

Here's a timeline of the band:

  • 1996 - Skillet was formed in Memphis, Tennessee, by John Cooper (lead vocals, bass) and Ken Steorts (guitar).
  • 1996 - Released their self-titled debut album Skillet, introducing their raw, grunge-influenced Christian rock sound.
  • 1998 - Released their second album Hey You, I Love Your Soul, marking a shift toward a more industrial and electronic sound.
  • 2000 - Ken Steorts left the band and was replaced by Kevin Haaland as the guitarist. Released the album Invincible with a more electronic rock sound, and Korey Cooper officially joined the band.
  • 2001 - Released Alien Youth, refining their hard rock style. The title track became a Christian rock anthem.
  • 2003 - Released Collide, which gained attention in both Christian and secular rock scenes.
  • 2006 - Released the album Comatose, which included hits like "Whispers in the Dark" and "Rebirthing," gaining critical acclaim.
  • 2008 - Comatose earned a Grammy nomination for Best Rock or Rap Gospel Album.
  • 2009 - Released Awake, which became their biggest commercial success, debuting at No. 2 on the Billboard 200. The singles “Monster” and “Hero” became massive hits.
  • 2013 - Released Rise, a concept album telling a story of overcoming life's challenges.
  • 2016 - Released Unleashed, featuring hits like “Feel Invincible” and “Stars.” The album hit No. 3 on the Billboard 200.
  • 2019 - Released Victorious, which showcased their powerful anthemic sound and included singles like “Legendary” and “Save Me.”
  • 2020 - Released an expanded edition of Victorious called Victorious: The Aftermath with additional tracks and remixes.
  • 2022 - Released their eleventh studio album, Dominion, continuing to emphasize their hard rock and faith-based themes.
  • Ongoing - Skillet continues to tour and release music, maintaining their status as a leading Christian rock band.
